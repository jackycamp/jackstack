The Man in the High Castle

PKD 1962

read june 2023 boston

In a reality where the Allies lost World War II, we peek into the lives of a few characters and catch a glimpse of a German and Japanese ruled society; the Germans occupying much of the eastern United States and Japanese the west.

First we are introduced to Richard Childan, who owns and runs the day-to-day of American Artistic Handcrafts Inc. a store dedicated to American "collectables" of which there seems to be a rather hot market for post WWII. Mr. Childan is your classic monetary obsessed (nickel-and-dime-you) sort of businessman.

Mr. Childan is anxiously waiting for an item to arrive of which a client of his is expecting; Nobusuke Tagomi. Mr. Tagomi is a bureaucrat at the Ranking Trade Mission in San Francisco, where he is a trade representative for the Japanese government. Mr. Tagomi is anticipating the arrival and meeting of his own client and wishes to procure a gift for him.

We pivot to Frank Frink, a factory worker, laying in bed mulling over his actions at work the day before that got him fired. He realizes his career is inevitably over since the industry will "blacklist" him and he is Jewish. In the midst of his existential crisis, he decides to consult the I Ching in order to figure out how to mend the relationship with his boss. But he also asks it about his ex-wife.

Skipping slightly ahead, we meet Juliana Frink, who is gazing at the sunset sky, waving, almost maliciously, at a Nazi transport rocket destined west. She lives in Colorado; a judo instructor. Juliana is a seemingly strong and independent woman but wanders her own life in search of answers.

Mr. Baynes, a Swedish gentleman, is on board a Nazi transport rocket (perhaps the one Juliana was waving to) headed to San Francisco. As the rocket starts its descent, Mr. Baynes converses with a young German man clearly revealing his distaste towards Germans. Mr. Tagomi greets Mr. Baynes when he lands and presents him with a 1938 Mickey Mouse wrist watch.

After being fired, one of his coworkers Ed McCarthy (and the I Ching) convinces Frank to start their own business EDFRANK Custom Jewelers. After two weeks they produce their first batch. Frank and Ed make their first selling attempt to Mr. Childan. Ed attempts the sell alone as earlier Frank went into the shop under a guise and reveals that Childan is selling non-authentic items. After some tactful manipulation on Childan's part and awkward salesmanship on Ed's, Childan convinces Ed to leave the items on consignment.

Some time passes, Frank and Ed fail to sell any products causing Frank to become doubtful of the venture. Frank wants to quit, Ed tries to convince him otherwise. After their dispute, Frank steps out for a cigarette, signaling defeat. A plain clothes police officer approaches Frank, presents documentation, reveals that Frank is a Jew, and utlimately arrests him. One of the police officers explains they will hold him for the German Consul to try him under German Law.

Later, due to Mr. Tagomi intervening (unbeknown to Frank), Frank is released from jail. Frank is in disbelief as he expected to be sent for extermination. Puzzled and on a completely different path than expected, he contemplates what to do next. Without consulting the I Ching, he rushes back to the shop, where Ed is having dinner. After a brief greeting back into the comprehensible, Frank lays down his coat, picks up some unfinished silver pieces, and continues his work.

Mr. Baynes informs Mr. Tagomi of a third party who will join their meeting.
